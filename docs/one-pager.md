# One-page guide: turn messy context into the next move

Use this when a Slack thread, email chain, meeting transcript, or doc comment has signal, but not yet a clean ask.

## Manager mode

- Situation: what happened, who is involved, and why it matters now
- Decision needed: what choice is still open
- Risk: what breaks, stalls, or creates confusion
- Owner: who should own the next step if known
- Suggested response: a short reply that moves the thread forward

Copy/paste prompt:

```text
Use manager mode. Give me the situation, the decision needed, the risk, the owner if known, and a suggested response. Keep it source-grounded and note any uncertainty.
```

## IC mode

- What changed: what is new or different
- What I owe: the reply, update, or action I need to deliver
- What I should ask: the missing question that clears the path
- Draft reply: a message I can send or adapt

Copy/paste prompt:

```text
Use IC mode. Tell me what changed, what I owe, what I should ask, and draft a reply. Keep it brief and do not invent owners or decisions.
```

## Exec skim mode

- Decision: the call that still needs to be made
- Impact: why it matters to the business
- Risks: what could go wrong
- Unresolved questions: what still needs confirmation
- Recommended next sentence: the next line I can say out loud or paste

Copy/paste prompt:

```text
Use exec skim mode. Give me the decision, impact, risks, unresolved questions, and one recommended next sentence. Keep it concise and uncertainty-aware.
```

## What good looks like

- Source-grounded
- Action-oriented
- Uncertainty-aware
- Politically sane
- Brief

## Bad output anti-patterns

- Fake certainty
- Smoothing over disagreement
- Inventing owners
- Turning every thread into a project plan
- Over-polishing a human reply
- Sending or mutating without explicit user request

## Quick rule

If the output does not make the next move clearer, it is too much summary and not enough conversation state.
