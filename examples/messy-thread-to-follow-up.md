# Messy Thread to Follow-Up Example

## Situation

A Slack thread is circling a customer-facing release note for a product tweak.

## Messy Input

```text
Alex: We should probably send the customer note tomorrow morning once the banner is approved.
Sam: I think we can send today and patch the help center later.
Priya: Support is already asking whether enterprise accounts are included in the change.
Jordan: Legal still hasn't signed off on the banner copy.
Alex: If we wait too long, sales will think we missed the window.
Sam: The wording is small; I don't think it should block the email.
Priya: Let's not say "fully live" to customers until the backend toggle is on.
Jordan: The toggle will be ready tonight, but the copy may need another pass.
Alex: Can someone handle this? I need to tell support what to expect.
Sam: My vote is to move now and send the FAQ after.
Priya: That could confuse the enterprise team if they get the email before support is ready.
Jordan: We need a call before 3pm because the send queue closes then.
Alex: Are we leaning toward tomorrow if legal doesn't come back in time?
Sam: Maybe, but nobody's owned the final call.
```

## Skills demonstrated

- `brief-me`
- `clear-ask`
- `meeting-to-actions`
- `follow-up-draft`

## 1. What changed

- The team may be leaning toward tomorrow if legal does not come back in time, but the final call is not explicit.
- The help center copy and banner still need to match.
- Support needs a clear heads-up about enterprise accounts.
- The final call is still not named.

## 2. Clean ask

Decide whether the customer note goes today or tomorrow, and name who owns the final call.

## 3. Decision needed

Should the team wait for legal sign-off and send tomorrow, or send today and follow up the FAQ later?

## 4. Owners and open loops

- Missing owner for the final decision.
- FAQ update remains unowned if tomorrow wins.
- Priya needs a support-ready answer on enterprise wording.
- Legal still needs to approve the banner copy.
- The send queue closes at 3pm.

## 5. Risks / blockers

- Legal approval is still pending.
- Sending before support is ready could confuse enterprise customers.
- The banner, email, and help center could drift out of sync.

## 6. Tone risk

- If the reply sounds final before legal signs off, it could overpromise and spook support.

## 7. Suggested follow-up draft

```text
Thanks all. We’re close, but we still need one clear call: do we send the customer note today or wait for legal and move it to tomorrow? The final owner isn’t named yet, and support needs a clear answer on enterprise wording before we lock it. Can someone confirm the decision owner and the timing before the 3pm send cutoff? If we wait, the FAQ still needs an owner.
```

## 8. Source gaps

- The decision owner is not named.
- Legal’s sign-off timing is unknown.
- It’s unclear whether support has already been briefed.
- The exact enterprise wording is not finalized.

## Why this beats a generic summary

- It turns a noisy Slack thread into a clear ask, a pending decision, and a send-ready reply.
- It keeps the owner gap and support sensitivity visible.
- It avoids pretending the decision is already made.
